ABOUT THE ADIVASI STORE
Woven from our roots

The Adivasi Store is a celebration of traditional hand-woven attire, crafted by indigenous artisans whose skills have travelled through stories, seasons and generations. Every piece is a quiet bridge between village looms and urban wardrobes.

OUR STORY

From handloom courtyards to your home

The Adivasi Store began as a small initiative to document and support the textiles that shaped our childhood – shawls, gamusas, wraps and sarees that carried the warmth of festivals, harvests and everyday life. Instead of letting these weaves disappear into memory, we chose to build a space where they could be seen, treasured and fairly valued.

Every collection is sourced directly from artisan families and women’s groups. We focus on slow-made pieces: hand-spun yarns, naturally leaning colours and motifs that hold cultural meanings. Your purchase keeps looms active, skills alive and local economies a little more secure.

FOUNDER & CUSTODIAN

Meet Vishal Nag

Electrical Engineer · Margherita, Assam, India

The Adivasi Store is founded by Vishal Nag, an electrical engineer from Margherita, Assam, who grew up surrounded by the colours, rhythms and textiles of tea-garden life and Adivasi neighbourhoods.

With a technical mind and a deep emotional connection to his roots, Vishal brings together precision, structure and community memory. The store is his way of ensuring that the designs he saw in childhood – shawls on washing lines, handwoven wraps at festivals, everyday workwear in the tea gardens – continue to have a future.

Between sourcing, quality checks, artisan conversations and late-night calls with the tech team, he keeps one thing constant: that every piece you order carries dignity, fair value and a story worth wearing.

THE PERSON BEHIND THE LOOM
TEA TRIBE COMMUNITY OF ASSAM

Where looms sit beside tea gardens

The Adivasi Store is deeply inspired by the Tea Tribe communities of Assam – families whose histories are woven into the state’s tea estates, yet whose textiles, songs and stories often stay invisible outside the gardens. Many of the silhouettes, motifs and colour combinations you see here are rooted in their way of dressing, working and celebrating.

WEAVES, WORK & WEEKENDS From early-morning leaf plucking to evening adda in workers’ lines, textiles move with the rhythm of the tea gardens – sturdy wraps for work, brighter shawls for festivals, everyday pieces that carry both comfort and identity. Our goal is to gently translate that world into garments that travel further, without losing their origin.

WHAT WE STAND FOR

Comfort, culture & care in every weave

01

Fair partnerships

We work with artisan clusters through transparent pricing, advance payments on bigger orders and long-term relationships, not one-time purchases.

02

Thoughtful design

Our styles are rooted in traditional motifs but cut for everyday wear – so you can reach for your handwoven pieces as often as your favourite T-shirt.

03

Slow, not seasonal

We release small curated drops instead of high-volume collections. This keeps production manageable for artisans and special for you.

OUR JOURNEY

How a piece travels to you

Loomside

Designs are co-created with artisans – respecting traditional motifs while gently adapting lengths, fits and colour palettes to how you wear them today.

Care & finishing

Each piece is washed, checked and finished in small batches. We keep packaging minimal – focusing on protection, not plastic.

To your wardrobe

Orders are shipped with clear care notes, so your garment can be worn for years, repaired when needed and passed on when you are ready.

PEOPLE & COMMUNITY

Built by a small team, held by many hands

Behind The Adivasi Store is a tiny team handling design, packaging, technology and everyday chaos – but the heart of this work will always be the artisan families, weavers’ collectives and communities who trust us with their craft. Thank you for walking this journey with us, one handwoven piece at a time.
