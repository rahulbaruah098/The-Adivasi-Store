ABOUT THE ADIVASI STORE
Woven from our roots

The Adivasi Store is a celebration of traditional hand-woven attire, crafted by indigenous artisans whose skills have travelled through stories, seasons and generations. Every piece is a quiet bridge between village looms and urban wardrobes.

OUR STORY

From handloom courtyards to your home

The Adivasi Store began as a small initiative to document and support the textiles that shaped our childhood — shawls, gamusas, wraps and sarees that carried the warmth of festivals, harvests and everyday life. Instead of letting these weaves disappear into memory, we chose to build a space where they could be seen, treasured and fairly valued.

Every collection is sourced directly from artisan families and women’s groups. We focus on slow-made pieces: hand-spun yarns, naturally leaning colours and motifs that hold cultural meanings. Your purchase keeps looms active, skills alive and local economies a little more secure.

Our promise is simple: when you open a parcel from us, you’re not just receiving clothing — you’re receiving a lineage of hands, a rhythm of the loom, and a story that deserves to travel.

FOUNDER & CUSTODIAN

Meet Vishal Nag

Electrical Engineer · Margherita, Assam, India

The Adivasi Store is founded by Vishal Nag, an electrical engineer from Margherita, Assam — someone who grew up surrounded by the colours, rhythms and textiles of tea-garden life and Adivasi neighbourhoods.

With a technical mind and a deep emotional connection to his roots, Vishal brings together precision, structure and community memory. The store is his way of ensuring that the designs he saw in childhood — shawls on washing lines, handwoven wraps at festivals, everyday workwear in the tea gardens — continue to have a future.

Today, the work is simple but constant: sourcing with respect, checking quality with care, and keeping the conversation open with the artisans so every order carries dignity, fair value, and a story worth wearing.

TEA TRIBE COMMUNITY OF ASSAM

Where looms sit beside tea gardens

The Adivasi Store is deeply inspired by the Tea Tribe communities of Assam — families whose histories are woven into the state’s tea estates, yet whose textiles, songs and stories often stay invisible outside the gardens. Many of the silhouettes, motifs and colour combinations you see here are rooted in their way of dressing, working and celebrating.

WEAVES, WORK & WEEKENDS From early-morning leaf plucking to evening adda in workers’ lines, textiles move with the rhythm of the tea gardens — sturdy wraps for work, brighter shawls for festivals, everyday pieces that carry both comfort and identity.

We don’t “modernise” a culture. We translate it gently — keeping origin visible, keeping craft respected, and ensuring the makers are paid fairly for what they create.
